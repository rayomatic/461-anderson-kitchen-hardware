## Design Decisions Made (Final)

1. **Brand: Emtek Satin Brass (US4)** — switched from original Top Knobs Honey Bronze to better match Grohe Minta faucet (Brushed Cool Sunrise). Consolidates with Emtek door hardware (Orb knob in ORB) already specified for the home.

2. **Hardware family:**
   - **Paxton round knob** (Timeless Classics Collection) for ALL upper cabinet doors and the sitting cabinet — transitional, warm, universally readable.
   - **Trail bar pulls** (Modern Collection) for all drawers and lower-cabinet vertical pulls — modern, clean, multiple sizes available.
   - Two collections work together because the Satin Brass finish is identical.

3. **Pull sizing — applied 1/3 rule (pull length ≈ 1/3 of drawer/door width):**
   - 3" pulls: 3DB12 (12" wide drawers)
   - 5" pulls: B12-PULL, B18-PULL pull-outs
   - 8" pulls: standard drawers (15–27" wide), all base-cabinet vertical door pulls
   - 12" pulls: pantry bottom doors (66" tall, ergonomic mid-height placement)

4. **Pull placement:**
   - Drawers: pull centered horizontally on drawer face
   - Lower doors with vertical pulls: pull mounted at TOP of door (natural reach)
   - Tall pantry doors (66"): pull mounted at MID-HEIGHT (~38–50" off floor for natural reach)
   - Knobs on upper doors: lower-inside corner (where hand naturally lands)
   - Knobs on lower doors (sitting cabinet): upper-inside corner

5. **What we considered and rejected:**
   - **Vertical pulls on navy uppers** — visually busy, hardware story too complex.
   - **Trail T-Knob** — too modernist for the warm transitional palette; subtle T-shape can read as "miniature pull" rather than knob.
   - **All-pulls / no-knobs** — too aggressive contemporary, loses warmth.
   - **5" verticals at bottom of upper doors** — looked stubby on tall doors, crowded short doors.
